# The Last Performance at Bellwether Hall

**Campaign:** Bellwether Mysteries  
**Tradition:** Community cozy  
**System focus:** Stage geography, cues, and conflicting eyewitness accounts

## Campaign role

This middle case unlocks with `A Recipe for Silence` after the introductory case
and may be played before or after it. Bellwether Hall persists in the hub before
and after the murder. Completion contributes one of the two requirements for
`Death on the Promenade`; its survivors later attend the waterfront festival.

## Premise

During the final rehearsal of a neighborhood play, director Celia North falls
from the fly gallery. The cut safety line suggests sabotage during rehearsal,
but the fatal injury occurred earlier backstage.

## Cast

- **Celia North, victim:** Director planning to replace the hall's artistic team.
- **Marcus Vale, culprit:** Technical director whose dangerous embezzlement Celia uncovered.
- **Ruth Anwar, suspect:** Lead actor hiding a relationship with Celia.
- **Theo Marsh, suspect:** Young playwright who stole material from an unproduced script.

## Five investigative locations

Auditorium, stage, scene dock, dressing room, and fly gallery. Curtains and cue
lights create changing sightlines; ladders and stairs establish travel time.

## Canonical crime

Marcus kills Celia with a stage brace in the scene dock, carries her to the
gallery in a rolled backdrop, cuts the line, and releases the body on a lighting
cue. Ruth's secret meeting and Theo's theft produce false testimony.

## Evidence plan

1. Celia's fall injuries do not account for the wound behind her ear.
2. Blood is trapped in a hinge of the cleaned stage brace.
3. The rolled backdrop has a body-width crease and torn costume bead.
4. Dust on Celia's clothes matches the scene dock.
5. The cut line was severed before the rehearsal began.
6. The cue log was edited from Marcus's console.
7. Ruth heard Celia alive earlier but lies about why they met.
8. Theo's stolen pages place him in the dressing room during the move.
9. Marcus describes the body's position before the gallery lights reveal it.

## Required deductions and finale

Prove that the fall was staged, reconstruct the backdrop route, and explain the
false cue time. Marcus's premature knowledge of the hidden body position is the
decisive proof. The finale restages the lighting cue for the assembled company.

## Fairness notes

The audience can inspect both routes. The decisive statement is recorded as an
initial claim, so it cannot disappear behind a dialogue check.
