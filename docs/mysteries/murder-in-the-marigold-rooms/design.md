# Murder in the Marigold Rooms

**Campaign:** The Marigold Circle  
**Tradition:** Closed-circle reconstruction  
**System focus:** False time of death, body movement, and ensemble reveal

## Campaign role

This is episode one of a strict four-case sequence. It introduces the private
investigator, the Marigold Club, its foundation, and recurring members of its
social circle. Completion alone unlocks `Five Places at Dinner`.

Surviving characters and established public facts may recur, but no clue is
required outside this case.

## Premise

At a private dinner in the Marigold Club, financier Ambrose Vane announces that
he will expose corruption in the club's charitable foundation. Later he is found
stabbed in the library after guests hear him arguing behind its closed door.

## Cast

- **Ambrose Vane, victim:** Financier who used the foundation records as leverage.
- **Clara Wren, culprit:** Foundation director who diverted funds to conceal an earlier fraud.
- **Basil Trent, suspect:** Heir secretly negotiating to sell family property.
- **Sonia Pell, suspect:** Club secretary who forged Ambrose's signature on a personal loan.

## Five investigative locations

Dining room, library, conservatory, service corridor, and cloakroom. Public and
service routes support competing timelines.

## Canonical crime

Clara kills Ambrose in the conservatory before dinner, moves him through the
service corridor on a linen trolley, and stages the library scene beside a
stopped clock. A recording plays their earlier argument. Basil and Sonia lie to
protect their unrelated secrets.

## Evidence plan

1. Conservatory soil and blood identify the true murder place.
2. Trolley fibers and a damaged wheel show the body route.
3. The library blood pattern formed after the body was placed.
4. The clock was stopped by hand before the staged attack.
5. The recorded voice repeats with an identical background chime.
6. Clara's phonograph cabinet contains the missing recording sleeve.
7. Basil's sale papers explain his meeting and exclude him during movement.
8. Sonia's forged loan explains her access to Ambrose's papers.
9. Clara quotes a phrase from the muffled recording before it is played clearly.

## Required deductions and finale

Disprove the voice and clock, locate the murder in the conservatory, and trace
the trolley route. The detective reconstructs all three false assumptions before
the dinner party. Clara's unique knowledge of the recorded phrase is decisive.

## Fairness notes

Each assumption has physical and testimonial support. No conclusion depends on
recognizing a recording by sound alone.
