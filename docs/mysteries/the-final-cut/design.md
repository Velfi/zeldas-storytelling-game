# The Final Cut

**Campaign:** One More Question  
**Tradition:** Inverted mystery  
**System focus:** Known culprit, evolving confrontation, and proof by unique knowledge

## Campaign role

This is one of three anthology episodes available from the start and may be
played in any order. Completion marks one part of the campaign's `all_of` gate.
No clue, disposition, or deduction transfers to another episode; only completion,
performance records, and a display item persist in the police-office frame.

## Premise

The opening shows editor Vera Sloane kill her producing partner, Leon Pike, in a
private screening room and alter a workprint to create a false time of death.
The investigation asks how she did it and what mistake can prove it.

## Cast

- **Leon Pike, victim:** Producer preparing to expose fraudulent ownership records.
- **Vera Sloane, culprit:** Celebrated editor who kills to preserve her career and company.
- **Iris Chen, suspect:** Assistant concealing unauthorized use of studio footage.
- **Mal Reed, suspect:** Projectionist who runs an illicit after-hours film club.

## Five investigative locations

Screening room, projection booth, editing suite, film vault, and studio corridor.
Door acoustics, reel transport, and projector noise are physically testable.

## Canonical crime

Vera kills Leon in the screening room, removes a scene from the workprint, and
later plays Leon's recorded voice behind the booth door. She claims to recognize
a particular scene and to hear Leon respond while the projector runs.

## Evidence plan

1. Projector noise makes the claimed conversation unintelligible.
2. Leon's blood lies beneath a seat moved before the alleged death time.
3. The workprint contains a fresh splice and missing frames.
4. Film dust from the removed section remains in Vera's editing bin.
5. The vault checkout time contradicts Vera's account.
6. Iris's copied footage explains her access without giving murder opportunity.
7. Mal's club explains his false booth log.
8. Vera describes an image absent from the version she claims to have watched.
9. The removed frames are found inside a mislabeled reel Vera alone handled.

## Required deductions and finale

Prove the conversation impossible, establish the film as the false clock, and
show that Vera edited it after the murder. The detective screens the version
Vera claims to know; her description of the absent image is the decisive trap.

## Authoring requirements

The prologue must reveal Vera without granting player evidence. Culprit selection
should be replaced by required questions for method, contradiction, and proof.
Confrontations should reopen through authored evidence, not repeated checks.
The episode must launch cleanly without assuming another open case is complete.
