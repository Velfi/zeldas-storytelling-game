# The Last Garden Prize

**Campaign:** Bellwether Mysteries  
**Tradition:** Community cozy  
**System focus:** Social secrets, movement of a body, and environmental evidence

## Campaign role

This is the required Bellwether introduction. Completion opens the persistent
neighborhood hub and unlocks `The Last Performance at Bellwether Hall` and `A
Recipe for Silence` simultaneously. The community house remains visitable, with
its exhibition display and surviving residents updated after the case.

## Premise

Hours before Bellwether's annual flower exhibition, Garden Society president
Mara Voss is found dead inside the locked conservatory of the neighborhood's
historic community house. A silver prize cup lies beside her. The apparent
locked-room killing is a staging meant to implicate the only rival believed to
hold the spare key.

## Cast

- **Mara Voss, victim:** Exacting society president who discovered missing grant money.
- **Jon Bell, culprit:** Treasurer who diverted the money to save his failing nursery.
- **Nadia Price, suspect:** Rival gardener publicly threatened by Mara; secretly meeting Mara's spouse.
- **Eli Ward, suspect:** Groundskeeper who falsified pesticide records after damaging the beds.

## Five investigative locations

Community hall, conservatory, loading garden, potting shed, and exhibition lawn.
The cart route links the real murder site in the loading garden to the staged
scene. Steamed glass limits sightlines between the hall and conservatory.

## Canonical crime

Mara confronts Jon in the loading garden before dawn. He strikes her with a
cast-iron hose guide, hides it in a soil bin, lays her body on a plant cart, and
moves her into the conservatory. He places the prize cup, locks the public door,
and leaves through the service door. Nadia lies about her arrival to conceal her
affair; Eli lies about entering the shed to conceal his record tampering.

## Evidence plan

1. Soil on Mara's shoes matches the loading garden, not the conservatory beds.
2. Parallel wheel marks continue beneath the conservatory mat.
3. A bent cart latch bears a thread from Mara's coat.
4. The cup lacks the impact damage expected of the weapon.
5. Blood and hair remain on the hose guide in the soil bin.
6. The prize orchid was watered after Jon's claimed departure.
7. Jon alone knew the service-door latch could be lifted without the spare key.
8. Nadia's message establishes her secret meeting and removes her murder opportunity.
9. Eli's pesticide log explains his lie and excludes him from the body-moving interval.

## Required deductions and finale

Demonstrate that Mara died in the loading garden, that a cart moved her, and
that the trophy and locked door staged Nadia's guilt. The decisive proof combines
the watering time with Jon's unique knowledge of the service latch. The reveal
occurs while the exhibition is assembled, using the cart and door physically.

## Fairness notes

The weapon, route, and latch are all available without checks. Failed social
checks may delay the two unrelated secrets but cannot block their alternate
physical or documentary routes.
