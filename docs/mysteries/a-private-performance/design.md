# A Private Performance

**Campaign:** One More Question  
**Tradition:** Inverted mystery  
**System focus:** Recorded time, acoustic space, and conversational pressure

## Campaign role

This is one of three anthology episodes available from the start and may be
played in any order. Completion marks one part of the `all_of` gate for `The
Architect's Model`. It exports no evidence or required knowledge.

## Premise

The opening shows conductor Helena March kill orchestra trustee Simon Rusk in a
rehearsal studio, then play an edited rehearsal recording through the hall to
make him seem alive during her public conducting session.

## Cast

- **Simon Rusk, victim:** Trustee threatening to expose Helena's kickback scheme.
- **Helena March, culprit:** Celebrated conductor guarding her appointment and reputation.
- **Oren Bell, suspect:** Principal violinist concealing a contract breach.
- **Mina Shah, suspect:** Recording engineer who pirated private rehearsal tracks.

## Five investigative locations

Concert hall, rehearsal studio, control room, conductor's office, and instrument
store. Acoustic bleed and travel time connect them.

## Canonical crime

Helena kills Simon with a heavy metronome, hides him in the instrument store,
and inserts his earlier spoken interruption into a rehearsal recording. After
the public session she returns the body to the studio and stages a quarrel.

## Evidence plan

1. Simon's wound matches the office metronome.
2. Rosin on his coat comes from the instrument-store floor.
3. A wheel track shows the bass case used to move him.
4. The recorded interruption lacks the hall's live ventilation hum.
5. Its waveform contains an edit Mina can explain but did not make.
6. Helena's marked score contains the exact false interruption cue.
7. Oren's contract meeting explains his secret absence.
8. Mina's piracy explains her deleted recording log.
9. Helena recalls a dropped mute that occurred only after the source recording.

## Required deductions and finale

Prove the voice was recorded, reconstruct the bass-case route, and date the
inserted speech. A playback mixes the interruption with the later dropped mute;
Helena corrects the sequence, revealing knowledge unavailable from the alleged
live performance.

## Authoring requirements

Audio clues require readable visual equivalents—waveform, cue marks, and room
hum—so the case remains accessible without specialist listening ability.
The police-office frame may acknowledge completed episodes, but the case must
also work as the player's first selection.
