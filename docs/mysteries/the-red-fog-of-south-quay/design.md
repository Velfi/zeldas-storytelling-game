# The Red Fog of South Quay

**Campaign:** The Blackthorn Papers  
**Tradition:** Forensic adventure  
**System focus:** Playable precedent, residue transport, and rationalized menace

## Campaign role

This earlier paper unlocks when the carriage investigation finds conspicuous
brake residue that does not explain Mae's death. It may be played in any order
or left optional. Completion counts toward the `two_of_three` requirement.

The paper returns one campaign deduction: **The most visible physical effect may
be a deliberate distraction.** It transfers no South Quay evidence.

## Premise

Engineer Tomas Rell dies inside a locked pumping station as a rust-red fog fills
South Quay. Locals blame a toxic industrial release, but the visible fog is
harmless mineral dust used to conceal a targeted gas exposure indoors.

## Cast

- **Tomas Rell, victim:** Engineer who found falsified emissions controls.
- **Asha Venn, culprit:** Plant chemist who signed the false certification.
- **Kerr Moss, suspect:** Union organizer hiding damage from an unauthorized shutdown.
- **Lena Pike, suspect:** Wildlife rescuer who trespassed to remove affected animals.

## Five investigative locations

Pumping station, control room, pipe gallery, quay yard, and wildlife shed.
Fans, vents, and wind direction define the causal route.

## Canonical crime

Asha meets Tomas in the pumping station, releases a small quantity of lethal gas
through a maintenance line, locks the room remotely, and starts a yard blower
loaded with red iron dust. The spectacle suggests a broad accidental release and
obscures the maintenance-line residue.

## Evidence plan

1. Red dust is inert iron oxide and absent from Tomas's lungs.
2. Lethal residue concentrates around the maintenance vent.
3. Fan positions would push yard fog away from the victim.
4. A corroded valve was recently replaced and kept by Asha.
5. Remote-control logs show a manual sequence rather than an automated fault.
6. Caged birds in the wildlife shed remained healthy during the red fog.
7. Kerr's shutdown damage explains his false control-room statement.
8. Lena's trespass explains missing animals and her hidden route.
9. Asha warns of a gas that instruments never detected in the yard.

## Required deductions and finale

Separate visible dust from lethal gas, reconstruct airflow, and trace the targeted
line. A harmless smoke demonstration shows the yard cloud could not enter the
station. Asha's unique advance knowledge of the actual gas and replaced valve is decisive.

## Fairness notes

The case teaches every chemical distinction through residue, animals, instruments,
and airflow. No player chemistry knowledge is assumed.
