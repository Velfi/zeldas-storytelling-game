# Death on the Promenade

**Campaign:** Bellwether Mysteries  
**Tradition:** Community cozy  
**System focus:** Crowd movement, public sightlines, and civic conflict

## Campaign role

This is Bellwether's finale and unlocks only after both middle cases. The festival
gathers recurring survivors and reflects changes made around the persistent hub.
Prior cases alter attendance, ambient dialogue, and optional assistance, but all
evidence required to solve Owen's murder is contained here.

## Premise

Preservation campaigner Owen Lark is found beneath the old promenade during a
waterfront festival. Witnesses saw him arguing at the bandstand near the alleged
time of death, but they saw a distinctive coat rather than Owen himself.

## Cast

- **Owen Lark, victim:** Activist threatening to expose a fraudulent redevelopment survey.
- **Sabine Cross, culprit:** Survey consultant whose forged report approved demolition.
- **Dario Finch, suspect:** Food-stall owner facing eviction and heard threatening Owen.
- **Mei Rowan, suspect:** Festival organizer who concealed unsafe crowd permits.

## Five investigative locations

Bandstand, market arcade, preservation booth, service alley, and lower promenade.
Festival banners, stairs, and the tide govern visibility and travel.

## Canonical crime

Sabine meets Owen in the service alley, strikes him with a survey weight, and
pushes his body down a maintenance ramp to the lower promenade. She wears his
bright coat at the bandstand to manufacture a later sighting, then hides it in
the preservation booth.

## Evidence plan

1. Owen drowned only after receiving the fatal head injury.
2. Drag grit on his shoes matches the maintenance ramp.
3. The survey weight has been wiped but retains blood inside its eyelet.
4. Witnesses identify the coat and posture, never Owen's face.
5. A photograph shows the impersonator wearing Sabine's distinctive festival wristband.
6. The hidden coat contains fibers from Sabine's survey case.
7. Dario's till record and customers cover the movement interval.
8. Mei altered crowd counts, explaining her deleted messages.
9. Sabine knows the tide had not reached the body when she claims to discover it.

## Required deductions and finale

Establish the real death site, disprove the bandstand sighting, and reconstruct
the ramp route. The wristband comparison plus Sabine's unique tide knowledge
provides decisive proof. The reveal uses the festival photograph and sightline.

## Fairness notes

The crowd does not function as random noise: three authored statements share
the same limited observation, and the photograph supplies an independent route.
