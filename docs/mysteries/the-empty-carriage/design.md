# The Empty Carriage

**Campaign:** The Blackthorn Papers  
**Tradition:** Forensic adventure  
**System focus:** Suspended master case, `two_of_three` precedents, and mechanical transformation

## Campaign role

This is both the campaign entry point and the master case. The player investigates
Mae's disappearance until particular evidence opens three earlier Blackthorn
Papers as complete playable flashbacks. The master case remains suspended—with
location, discoveries, questions, and action economy preserved—while a paper is
active.

Any two completed papers unlock enough campaign deductions to finish the murder.
Completing all three unlocks an additional demonstration, the most complete final
explanation, and the highest campaign resolution. The papers may be entered in
any order whenever their triggering evidence has been found.

## Premise

Transit engineer Mae Corbin enters a sealed maintenance carriage inside a depot.
When its doors reopen, the carriage is empty. Her body is later found beneath a
different car, although no vehicle supposedly moved.

The detective recognizes several classes of deception but asks the companion to
consult earlier case notes. Those papers teach the player how this partnership
turns singular observations into general investigative principles.

## Cast

- **Mae Corbin, victim:** Engineer who discovered falsified brake inspections.
- **Galen Shore, culprit:** Depot contractor responsible for the forged certifications.
- **Tari Noll, suspect:** Driver who concealed an earlier unauthorized train movement.
- **Esme Park, suspect:** Photographer trespassing to document abandoned tunnels.

## Five investigative locations

Inspection carriage, maintenance pit, switch booth, tool bay, and wash track.
Floor hatches, rails, and short hidden movements connect the apparent impossibility.

## Canonical crime

Galen kills Mae in the inspection carriage with a brake shoe, lowers her through
its floor hatch onto a wheeled pit cradle, and moves her beneath the wash-track
car. He shifts the inspection carriage one car length during a power interruption,
then returns it, making fixed cameras show the same exterior at both endpoints.

## Evidence plan

1. Mae's wound matches the cleaned brake shoe.
2. Hatch grease marks her coat and proves the downward route.
3. Pit-cradle wheels carry fibers and fresh wash-track grit.
4. Rail chalk marks show the carriage moved and returned.
5. Camera framing records car numbers poorly but preserves a changed window scratch.
6. Switch contacts show a brief manual movement during the outage.
7. Tari's earlier movement explains his altered log.
8. Esme's photographs explain her trespass and capture the original scratch position.
9. Galen states the hatch was bolted before investigators inspect its hidden broken bolt.

## Casebook unlocks

- Mechanically regular grease marks unlock `The Ashes of Blackthorn Lane`.
- A continuous but poorly framed camera view unlocks `The Violinist Without a Shadow`.
- Conspicuous brake residue that does not match the wound unlocks `The Red Fog of South Quay`.

Each paper remains a locally complete mystery. On return, its general proposition
appears as a campaign-level deduction eligible for master-case demonstrations.

## Required deductions and finale

Demonstrate the hatch-and-cradle transfer, prove the carriage moved, and use the
scratch comparison to defeat the camera alibi. Galen's knowledge of the concealed
broken bolt, together with his switch access, is decisive.

The required master reconstruction accepts any two of three campaign principles:

- A tool may manufacture an apparently natural trace.
- Continuous observation may preserve a mistaken identity.
- A conspicuous physical effect may distract from the operative mechanism.

Each principle has a distinct accepted route through the local evidence. The
third principle adds a supported section to the reveal rather than changing the
culprit or enabling an unsupported accusation.

## Fairness notes

The carriage does not need continuous train simulation. Authored transforms and
before/after evidence states can support the movement, while the physical route
remains consistent with The City's Y-up coordinate convention.

The runtime must support nested case state, returning to an exact suspension
point, campaign-level deductions, a `two_of_three` prerequisite, and a finale
quality tier. None of those deductions substitutes for proof against Galen.
