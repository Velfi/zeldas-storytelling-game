# The Ashes of Blackthorn Lane

**Campaign:** The Blackthorn Papers  
**Tradition:** Forensic adventure  
**System focus:** Playable precedent and a reproducible impossible footprint

## Campaign role

This is an earlier Blackthorn Paper entered as a playable flashback from `The
Empty Carriage`. Mechanical regularity in the carriage's grease traces prompts
the companion to open this account. It may be played first, second, third, or
left optional; completion counts toward the master case's `two_of_three` gate.

The paper returns one campaign deduction: **A trace can be manufactured by a
tool.** It transfers no character, object, or culprit evidence to the master case.

## Premise

Cartographer Arun Black is found dead in his locked upstairs workroom. Wet
footprints cross from the window to the body but never return. A burned map seems
to link the crime to a stolen municipal redevelopment plan.

## Cast

- **Arun Black, victim:** Cartographer who discovered a fraudulent land survey.
- **Cora Fen, culprit:** Survey chief whose false boundary would enrich a developer.
- **Milo Venn, suspect:** Apprentice who sold copies of unrelated city maps.
- **Dr. Inez Ward, suspect:** Preservationist who removed an archaeological field note.

## Five investigative locations

Street office, upstairs workroom, print room, rear courtyard, and survey store.
The window, stair, and roofline make the supposed entry route testable.

## Canonical crime

Cora enters normally, kills Arun with a brass divider, burns a tracing-linen
decoy, and stamps wet prints using a carved boot sole fixed to a survey staff.
She locks the workroom with Arun's key and returns it through the transom.

## Evidence plan

1. Footprint stride is mechanically exact and lacks heel-to-toe pressure.
2. The survey staff accepts the carved sole found in the store.
3. Clay in the prints comes from Cora's current survey site.
4. The window sill lacks entry disturbance.
5. The transom permits the key return but not bodily entry.
6. Ash fibers identify tracing linen rather than the original map.
7. Pressure impressions on the drawing board preserve the true boundary figures.
8. Milo's sales ledger explains his copied maps.
9. Inez's field note explains her visit and excludes her during the killing.

## Required deductions and finale

Reproduce the prints, explain the locked door, distinguish the burned decoy, and
recover the plan from impressions. The sole, site clay, and Cora's exclusive
control of that survey establish decisive opportunity. The finale performs the
footprint trick in front of the suspects.

## Fairness notes

Specialist conclusions are earned through comparison demonstrations. No fact is
announced solely because the detective possesses expert knowledge.
