# The Guest in Suite 808

**Campaign:** The Marigold Circle  
**Tradition:** Closed-circle reconstruction  
**System focus:** Adjoining rooms, assumed identity, and service circulation

## Campaign role

This is episode three, unlocked by `Five Places at Dinner`. It exposes the
historical and financial relationship between the Marigold Club, foundation,
and old hotel. Its conclusion directly schedules the private rail excursion but
does not export murder evidence to the finale.

## Premise

During a heritage-foundation gala at The City's oldest hotel, donor Lucien Grey
is found dead in Suite 808. Records show only Lucien entered, yet the dead man was
using another guest's identity and occupied the adjoining suite.

## Cast

- **Lucien Grey, victim:** Fixer using a donor's identity to retrieve incriminating letters.
- **Estelle Rook, culprit:** Hotel owner whose family purchased the hotel through coercion.
- **Nico Grey, suspect:** Lucien's brother, the registered guest, hiding from creditors.
- **Priya Sen, suspect:** Archivist who removed letters to protect a living subject.

## Five investigative locations

Suite 808, adjoining Suite 810, eighth-floor corridor, service lift, and hotel
archive. A connecting door and dumbwaiter create legitimate but concealed routes.

## Canonical crime

Estelle meets Lucien in 810, kills him with a marble bookend, and moves him through
the connecting door into 808. She dresses him in Nico's gala coat, sends the lift
down under Lucien's key, and relies on the register to establish identity and access.

## Evidence plan

1. Blood in 810 identifies the true murder room.
2. The cleaned bookend retains blood in its felt base.
3. Connecting-door paint flakes cling to the body's sleeve.
4. The key log records keys, not people.
5. The gala coat fits Nico and contains his claim ticket.
6. A room-service signature proves Lucien used Nico's name earlier.
7. Nico's debt papers explain the identity loan.
8. Priya's archive removal explains the missing letters.
9. Estelle knows the connecting door sticks at floor level although she denies entering either suite.

## Required deductions and finale

Correct the victim's assumed identity, locate the murder in 810, and demonstrate
the connecting-door transfer. Estelle's unique knowledge of the hidden door defect,
paired with paint evidence, supplies the decisive proof.

## Fairness notes

Identity is never solved from appearance alone. The coat, signature, claim ticket,
and room assignments give redundant routes.
