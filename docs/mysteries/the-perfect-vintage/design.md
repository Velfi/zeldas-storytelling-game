# The Perfect Vintage

**Campaign:** One More Question  
**Tradition:** Inverted mystery  
**System focus:** Environmental change and a culprit-authored discovery scene

## Campaign role

This is one of three anthology episodes available from the start. It may be the
player's first, second, or third case and supplies one completion flag for the
capstone gate. Only a case photograph or souvenir persists afterward.

## Premise

The opening shows collector-restaurateur Conrad Vey kill wine critic Elise Marr
after she discovers his celebrated cellar contains forged bottles. He stages her
death as a fall caused by a collapsing rack.

## Cast

- **Elise Marr, victim:** Critic preparing to publish proof of the fraud.
- **Conrad Vey, culprit:** Restaurateur whose identity and fortune depend on the collection.
- **Pia Soren, suspect:** Sommelier secretly selling small quantities from open bottles.
- **Luca Penn, suspect:** Cellar contractor concealing substandard rack repairs.

## Five investigative locations

Dining room, tasting room, main cellar, service cellar, and loading court.
Temperature zones, dust, and a narrow bottle lift define the physical puzzle.

## Canonical crime

Conrad strikes Elise with a bottle cradle in the tasting room, lowers her through
the service bottle lift, and places her beneath a loosened rack in the main
cellar. He moves a rare bottle beside her to establish a false discovery time.

## Evidence plan

1. Elise's head wound predates the rack injuries.
2. Blood remains inside the bottle cradle joint.
3. Her coat carries tasting-room carpet fibers.
4. The bottle lift contains a torn coat thread.
5. Condensation shows the rare bottle entered the main cellar recently.
6. Dust beneath the rack proves it was loosened before the claimed collapse.
7. Pia's sales explain the inventory discrepancy.
8. Luca's bad repairs explain his hidden access.
9. Conrad identifies the bottle as warm before anyone has touched it.

## Required deductions and finale

Establish the tasting room as the murder site, demonstrate the lift transfer,
and date the planted bottle through condensation. The detective substitutes an
identical properly chilled bottle; Conrad's insistence that it had been warm
proves his knowledge of the staging.

## Authoring requirements

Temperature must be communicated by visible condensation states and inspection
text rather than an invisible simulation. The final substitution is a Graph Mode
scene backed by already established deductions.
No interaction may assume evidence or deductions imported from another episode.
