# The Sleeper Across the City

**Campaign:** The Marigold Circle  
**Tradition:** Closed-circle reconstruction  
**System focus:** Moving locations, corridor timing, and exchanged compartments

## Campaign role

This is episode four and the fixed finale of the Marigold limited series. It
unlocks only after `The Guest in Suite 808` and gathers recurring survivors with
new suspects aboard the excursion. Earlier cases enrich dialogue and the final
reflection on institutional secrecy; Hugo's murder remains locally solvable.

## Premise

On a private overnight rail excursion around The City, philanthropist Hugo Quill
is found dead inside his locked sleeping compartment. The attendant saw him enter
and nobody saw him leave, but two passengers quietly exchanged compartments.

## Cast

- **Hugo Quill, victim:** Philanthropist blackmailing associates with foundation records.
- **Vivian Rohe, culprit:** Board member whose shell charity Hugo was about to expose.
- **Eamon Price, suspect:** Journalist traveling under another passenger's booking.
- **Leila Hart, suspect:** Singer concealing a private meeting with Hugo.

## Five investigative locations

Observation lounge, dining car, sleeping compartment, attendant vestibule, and
baggage car. The train loops within The City; only relative time and movement matter.

## Canonical crime

Vivian drugs Hugo in the lounge, takes him through the vestibule in a luggage
chair, and kills him in the baggage car. She returns the body to the compartment
registered to him, unaware Eamon and Hugo exchanged rooms. She locks it using the
attendant's service catch and falsifies a corridor sighting with Hugo's robe.

## Evidence plan

1. Baggage dust and rope fiber mark the true murder site.
2. Wheel grease on Hugo matches the luggage chair.
3. The compartment blood is transfer rather than attack spatter.
4. The service catch can lock a door from the corridor.
5. Ticket sleeves and luggage tags prove the room exchange.
6. A witness saw Hugo's robe, not his face.
7. Eamon's hidden identity explains the exchange.
8. Leila's note explains her private meeting and its timing.
9. Vivian names the registered compartment although she claims to have followed Hugo personally.

## Required deductions and finale

Establish the exchange, move the body by the only affordable route, and expose
the robe sighting. Vivian's knowledge of the intended rather than actual room is
decisive. The reveal uses a carriage diagram and reconstructed luggage-chair run.

## Fairness notes

The train need not move in simulation. Authored event times, blocked transitions,
and recurring window views communicate the journey.
