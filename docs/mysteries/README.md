# Mysteries of The City

This slate explores four detective traditions and four campaign forms inside one
contemporary, timeless City. Each folder contains a mystery design implemented
as an InteractiveStory document with a MysteryDomain payload: one victim, three suspects, five locations, 8–10 clues, a fair
decisive proof, and a winning route that does not depend on chance.

In these designs, “five locations” always means five connected investigative
spaces inside the case. It does not mean five city-map destinations. The city
map is shared and static: permanent landmarks are runtime-owned, while the
MysteryDomain payload may contribute a lightweight city-label ID for its venue.
That label occupies a reserved
city site and never creates geometry.
Police-station framing is optional; only a case that explicitly uses it should
begin there.

## Campaign structure matrix

| Campaign | Form | Progression rule | Persistence test |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Bellwether Mysteries | Persistent neighborhood hub | Intro, two middle cases in either order, finale | Shared world and relationships |
| One More Question | Gated anthology | Three open episodes; complete all three to unlock capstone | Order-independent `all_of` gate |
| The Marigold Circle | Linear limited series | Four cases in fixed order | Returning cast and authored pacing |
| The Blackthorn Papers | Nested casebook | Master case contains three playable precedents; any two suffice | Suspended case, reusable deductions, `two_of_three` gate |

## Bellwether Mysteries — community and character

- [The Last Garden Prize](the-last-garden-prize/design.md)
- [The Last Performance at Bellwether Hall](the-last-performance-at-bellwether-hall/design.md)
- [Death on the Promenade](death-on-the-promenade/design.md)

`The Last Garden Prize` introduces the persistent Bellwether hub. `The Last

Completing both unlocks the neighborhood finale, `Death on the Promenade`.

## One More Question — inverted mysteries

- [The Final Cut](the-final-cut/design.md)
- [The Architect's Model](the-architects-model/design.md)
- [A Private Performance](a-private-performance/design.md)
- [The Perfect Vintage](the-perfect-vintage/design.md)

The first, third, and fourth cases above are open anthology episodes playable in
any order. Completing all three unlocks the capstone, `The Architect's Model`.
No evidence transfers between episodes.

## The Marigold Circle — closed-circle reconstruction

- [Murder in the Marigold Rooms](murder-in-the-marigold-rooms/design.md)

## The Blackthorn Papers — observation and experiment

- [The Ashes of Blackthorn Lane](the-ashes-of-blackthorn-lane/design.md)
- [The Violinist Without a Shadow](the-violinist-without-a-shadow/design.md)
- [The Red Fog of South Quay](the-red-fog-of-south-quay/design.md)
- [The Empty Carriage](the-empty-carriage/design.md)

`The Empty Carriage` is the active master case and campaign entry point.
Evidence within it unlocks the other three cases as playable earlier papers.
Completing any two precedent cases supplies enough general deductions to finish
the master case; completing all three enables the fullest resolution.

## Campaign fairness boundary

Every murder remains locally solvable. Bellwether and Marigold continuity never
supplies required evidence. OMQ transfers no evidence at all. Blackthorn alone
transfers campaign deductions, and these are general principles earned from
complete precedent mysteries—not facts that identify the master-case culprit.

## Shared setting rules

The City has current technology but no fixed date. Avoid brands, topical events,
fashion-year shorthand, and solutions that depend on a character forgetting an
obvious modern tool. Phones, cameras, and access records may constrain a theory;
physical space, human knowledge, and reconstructable action provide the proof.

The four series reveal different layers of the same City. Bellwether shows its
neighborhoods, One More Question its accomplished elite, the Marigold Circle its
closed institutions, and the Blackthorn Papers its material secrets. Recurring
places may cross between campaigns, but each case must stand alone.
