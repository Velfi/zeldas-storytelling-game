# The Violinist Without a Shadow

**Campaign:** The Blackthorn Papers  
**Tradition:** Forensic adventure  
**System focus:** Playable precedent, reflections, and sightline experiments

## Campaign role

This earlier paper unlocks when depot-camera evidence in `The Empty Carriage`
appears continuous yet fails to identify the carriage precisely. It may be played
in any precedent slot or omitted. Completion counts toward `two_of_three`.

The paper returns one campaign deduction: **Continuous observation can preserve
a mistaken identity.** No evidence about Dorian or the arts center transfers.

## Premise

Violinist Mara Quill appears to vanish from the enclosed courtyard of a City arts
center while watched from three windows. Moments later she is found dead in a
locked practice room. Witnesses agree that her music continued throughout.

## Cast

- **Mara Quill, victim:** Soloist preparing to expose the sale of a donated instrument.
- **Dorian Kest, culprit:** Arts-center director who substituted a copy for the violin.
- **Sel Vale, suspect:** Rival soloist hiding an audition sabotage.
- **Pavel Orr, suspect:** Custodian who rents practice rooms unofficially after hours.

## Five investigative locations

Courtyard, practice room, recital hall, director's office, and glass passage.
Angled windows reflect the recital hall into the courtyard from selected positions.

## Canonical crime

Dorian records Mara's warm-up, lures her to the practice room, and kills her with
a music-stand weight. He plays the recording in the recital hall while a dressed
mannequin crosses the glass passage; reflections make it appear that Mara remains
in the courtyard before vanishing behind a column.

## Evidence plan

1. The courtyard sighting lacks a shadow because it is a reflection.
2. Sightline testing reveals the recital hall in the window glass.
3. The repeated musical phrase is acoustically identical.
4. Playback timing includes a bell that rang earlier.
5. Mannequin fibers and Mara's spare scarf remain in the passage.
6. The stand weight retains blood inside its adjustment collar.
7. Sel's sabotaged strings explain her hidden visit.
8. Pavel's rentals explain the falsified room log.
9. Dorian describes the reflection turning toward a window it could not see.

## Required deductions and finale

Demonstrate the reflected figure, prove the music was recorded, and locate the
real murder in the practice room. Recreating the reflection exposes the physical
impossibility in Dorian's account; the stolen violin in his office supplies motive.

## Fairness notes

Reflection states should be authored as clear scene changes rather than rely on
renderer-perfect optics. The sightline remains spatial and player-testable.
