# Five Places at Dinner

**Campaign:** The Marigold Circle  
**Tradition:** Closed-circle reconstruction  
**System focus:** Seating order, serving sequence, and targeted poisoning

## Campaign role

This is episode two and unlocks only after `Murder in the Marigold Rooms`. It
expands the club into its charitable foundation and collection. Returning
characters acknowledge the first investigation; completing this case unlocks
`The Guest in Suite 808`.

## Premise

At a foundation dinner, patron Imogen Saye dies after drinking from a glass that
several people handled. A last-minute place-card exchange turned a general table
setting into a precisely targeted murder.

## Cast

- **Imogen Saye, victim:** Patron preparing to remove the foundation's curator.
- **Felix Dorr, culprit:** Curator who sold collection pieces and replaced them with copies.
- **Alma Voss, suspect:** Beneficiary secretly related to Imogen.
- **Ren Park, suspect:** Caterer hiding an unlicensed side business.

## Five investigative locations

Reception room, dining room, serving pantry, terrace, and collection gallery.
Seat positions determine who can reach each glass and when.

## Canonical crime

Felix applies poison beneath the rim of the glass at Imogen's original place.
When Alma swaps two cards to sit beside Imogen, Felix privately restores the
glasses but not the cards. He later alters the printed seating chart and plants
poison in Ren's pantry kit.

## Evidence plan

1. Poison lies on one rim rather than in the shared wine.
2. Condensation rings show two glasses were moved after pouring.
3. Place cards bear Alma's fingerprints in the first arrangement.
4. Felix's chart has a fresh correction hidden beneath its frame.
5. Gallery-camera composition incidentally preserves the original table reflection.
6. Poison in Ren's kit was added after service began.
7. Alma's family letter explains the card swap.
8. Ren's invoices explain the unlicensed deliveries.
9. Felix describes Imogen drinking from the seat he claims was never hers.

## Required deductions and finale

Reconstruct the original seating, distinguish cards from glasses, and show the
poison was targeted before the swap. A physical reset of the table reveals that
Felix's eyewitness claim is possible only from knowledge of his own correction.

## Fairness notes

The player manipulates a five-place diagram through a comparison demonstration;
they are not expected to retain the whole service sequence from dialogue.
