# The Torn Appointment: Narrative North Star

## Player promise

In roughly one hour, the player enters a beautifully controlled household,
discovers that everyone has lied about the same dinner for a different reason,
and earns the pleasure of physically rejoining the torn appointment that breaks
the killer's exact denial.

The tone is an elegant drawing-room mystery with dry character comedy, tactile
investigation, and rising pressure. Edgar's death is serious; the fun comes from
out-observing people who believe precision can protect them.

## One-hour shape

- **0-5 minutes — The invitation:** learn the controls at the station, receive a
  dispatch built around one visual impossibility, and drive to Vale House.
- **5-15 minutes — The composed household:** meet Miriam, Daniel, and Elsie;
  inspect the body; hear three accounts that appear to support one another.
- **15-30 minutes — The false scene:** establish that the garden death was
  staged, locate the study as the murder room, and identify the statuette.
- **30-45 minutes — Lies with other purposes:** expose Daniel's affair and
  Elsie's theft. Both become plausible suspects before their lies turn into
  evidence against Miriam.
- **45-55 minutes — The torn appointment:** recover both fragments, join them,
  and confront Miriam with the exact sentence she cannot revise away.
- **55-60 minutes — The pleasure of explanation:** assemble the event chain and
  deliver a reveal that names not only the killer, but the purpose of every lie.

These are pacing bands, not gates. Players may discover evidence out of order;
the runtime should preserve the emotional sequence by making later reactions
acknowledge what the player already knows.

When several approaches become available at once, presentation order must
protect momentum. Evidence-producing checks come first, followed by questions
whose answers unlock another conversation, then highly specific late-case
confrontations. Optional recollections remain available on later pages. This is
especially important for a player who searches the whole house before beginning
interviews: thoroughness should create options, not bury the route forward.

## Character engines

- **Miriam controls by correcting.** She straightens objects, narrows questions,
  and edits other people's language. Her composure should fail through tiny
  imprecisions before it fails through volume.
- **Daniel protects by professionalizing.** He turns desire into procedure and
  cowardice into discretion. His driest lines should also incriminate him.
- **Elsie survives by inventorying.** She trusts duties, denominations, and the
  location of objects more than people. Her theft is real, sympathetic, and
  costly enough that confessing it feels like an act.
- **The detective notices before judging.** Their best lines isolate a detail or
  repeat a suspect's exact words. They should not announce deductions the player
  has not yet earned.
- **Edgar survives as a different memory in every witness.** Miriam remembers
  quiet corrections, Daniel remembers leverage, and Elsie remembers correctness
  offered in place of kindness. Recollections should humanize the victim while
  exposing the speaker's grievance; they must not turn cruelty into permission
  for murder. A player who hears all three can synthesize their shared pattern
  at the pond: Edgar made control look like order, while the suspects learned
  different mixtures of imitation and fear.

## Clue-flow rules

1. Every necessary conclusion needs at least two legible routes or one route
   that cannot be permanently lost.
2. Each paid action should change the player's model of the night, unlock a new
   lead, or materially change a character relationship.
3. Red herrings must resolve into useful truth. Daniel's affair explains the
   false alibi; Elsie's theft gives her the sightline that places Miriam.
4. Physical evidence answers **what happened**; conversation answers **why
   people lied**. Neither should merely repeat the other.
5. The torn note remains the signature payoff. References to paper, fire,
   handwriting, exact wording, and things left crooked may foreshadow it, but no
   earlier clue should perform its decisive contradiction.
6. Room descriptions and barks should point to oddities, never to the next
   required click.
7. A clue description names the examination the player is attempting; its
   proposition states the fact earned on success. Never put the successful
   conclusion on the pre-check card, and always show the proposition when the
   check resolves.

## Revision test

A pass improves the case when it strengthens at least one of these without
weakening the others: curiosity in the first five minutes, suspect ambiguity at
the midpoint, clarity of the crime sequence, emotional cost of a confession,
agency in forming deductions, or satisfaction of the final explanation.

For timing, count traversal, inspection, board work, and decision time—not only
spoken-word duration. A one-hour mystery should contain about 35-45 minutes of
critical-path play and 15-25 minutes of optional or order-dependent material.

## Current action-economy target

The twelve-tick budget should support a focused physical case with room to
follow the household's competing lies. The tested physical proof route spends
seven ticks on the ledger, memo stub, watch, statuette, cane, polishing cloth,
and disturbed dinner settings, leaving five ticks for social evidence, the
burned fragment, alternate routes, or one failed check. Free introductions,
follow-up dialogue, traversal, room searches, physical interactions, board
assembly, and the final reveal supply play time without punishing curiosity.

The cane, cloth, and statuette must remain separate observations. The cane
proves staging, the cloth proves cleaning, and the blood beneath the rug plus
the statuette's wound match prove the murder room and weapon. No single object
should collapse that whole reconstruction into one click.

The statuette conclusion also has an enforced internal order. The player must
lift the shifted rug to expose diluted blood and watch glass, then rotate the
freshly polished bronze until the missed blood-dark seam comes into view. Only
after both observations exist may the wound-match check establish the study and
statuette as place and weapon. The check may infer a relationship; it must not
invent either of its premises.

## Current pacing evidence

The authored case currently contains 38 playable scenes, 156 spoken lines
(about 2,500 words), 27 dialogue approaches, 11 clues, nine questions, nine
demonstrations, and six outcomes. Automated coverage exercises 16 routes across
228 scenario steps, including the complete opening handoff, the decisive paper confrontation, and a complete-investigation path that gathers every
clue and solves every question inside the twelve-tick budget.

The late-case paper trail should breathe before it resolves. Finding Edgar's
memo stub can lead to Daniel's explanation that Edgar retained appointment
roots as leverage; finding the burned half can lead to Elsie's observation that
Miriam normally destroyed private papers completely. These are optional
anticipation beats, not substitutes for joining the torn edges. They should add
roughly three to five minutes for a curious player while preserving the rejoined
appointment as the only decisive contradiction.

Once Daniel's affair and Elsie's theft are fully exposed, a curious player can
bring both witnesses to the cold dinner table. Miriam tries to turn their
dishonesty into reasonable doubt; Daniel refuses to let cowardice remain her
alibi, and Elsie refuses to let sixteen stolen pounds erase what she saw. This
ensemble beat should bridge the separate midpoint confessions into Act IV of the
reveal while allowing Miriam one last technically correct defense: suspicion is
not yet proof.

These counts support the one-hour shape but do not prove it. Completion requires
a timed, first-playthrough run that records arrival at each pacing band, time
spent navigating or reconsidering evidence, optional scenes encountered, and
the final outcome. Revise against observed dead air or rushed beats rather than
inflating word count to meet the clock.

## Outcome promise

Every ending should close the immediate character pressure, not merely grade the
player. An airtight account earns the full chronology. A correct but unproven
case should feel salvageable because Daniel and Elsie commit their statements;
an incomplete case should show Miriam regaining control of the record. A wrong
accusation buys her a credible escape, while silence or timeout lets each
witness leave with the secret the player failed to force into the room. Failure
copy must remain compatible with early and late exits and must never claim the
player discovered evidence they may not have found.
